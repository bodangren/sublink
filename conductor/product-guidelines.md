# Product Guidelines - SubLink\n\n## Prose & Communication\n- **Tone:** Pragmatic, direct, and non-bureaucratic. Avoid corporate jargon.\n- **Clarity First:** Instructions should be brief and actionable. Use common trade terms where appropriate (e.g., 'sub,' 'GC,' 'COI').\n\n## UX & Visual Design\n- **Rugged Aesthetic:** Use high-contrast colors (deep blues, safety oranges) for maximum readability on sun-glared construction sites.\n- **One-Tap Priority:** Core functions like 'Take Photo' or 'Sign Waiver' must be accessible with a single tap from the home screen.\n- **Feedback:** Provide immediate visual and haptic feedback for successful data saves, especially important for offline states.\n- **Accessibility:** Design for use with gloved hands (large touch targets) and outdoor environments.\n\n## Functional Priorities\n- **Offline Reliability:** The application MUST function without an internet connection. Data persistence is the highest technical priority.\n- **Data Integrity:** All photo evidence must include non-editable geospatial and time metadata to maintain its value as legal proof.
